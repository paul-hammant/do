Paul Hammant's DevOps

I’m an expert DevOps Assessor, and veteran of executive leadership for software teams, who has had plenty of development and architecture experience.

As a DevOps Assessor, I arrive at a corporate client site, ask a whole bunch of questions, look at systems, apps, infra, code, and come up with a recommendation plan for the client. I’m always going to focus on the lower tiers of the DevOps layer cake, because they facilitate more advanced aspects, and are almost always needing attention.

I’ve presented this findings to CEOs and CIOs, have seen clients say yes to everything or be paralyzed and unable to implement any changes. I’ve crafted my practice and honed my approach after tens of these assessments.

That is what I do today: go into established development organizations, perform DevOps assessments over a week, present the findings back to the exec leadership, including a suggested sequence for the changes to be be made to get improvements, and how to measure progress towards goals.

I have brief case studies for an airline, a FX trading bank, a fin-tech startup.

You should know that I:

  • lead the creation of the info portal TrunkBasedDevelopment.com
  • am the person who’s written most about Trunk-Based Development, generally
  • am the first person to write about Branch by Abstraction
  • co-created Selenium back in 2004
  • co-created the first Dependency Injection Container that used Constructor Injection in 2003
